How much depth and reads per cell?

The required depth for RNA-seq depends on the study‚Äôs goal, but a common range is 30-60 million reads per sample for a broad view of gene expression. If you are managing to sequence 10k cells, you will probably end up with 2000-3500 read pairs per cell.

Spatial transcriptomics technologies

What is it about?

Spatial transcriptomics allows researchers to quantify gene expression while preserving spatial context within a tissue section.

  • Each measurement is linked to a precise location on the tissue.
  • Combines histological imaging with RNA sequencing to map cellular functions in space.
  • Enables discovery of:
    • Tissue organization and cell interactions
    • Spatial gradients in gene expression
    • Regional responses to disease or injury

üß† Think of it as scRNA-seq + coordinates ‚Üí a map of molecular activity within tissues.

Overview

Visium (10x Genomics)

10x Genomics Visium is one of the most widely used spatial transcriptomics platforms.

  • Uses glass slides covered with 55 Œºm barcoded capture spots, each containing millions of oligo-dT probes.
  • Captures mRNA directly from tissue placed on the slide, maintaining spatial information via spot-specific barcodes.
  • Generates paired data:
    • Histological image (H&E stain)
    • Spatial gene expression matrix

üî¨ Resolution: ~1‚Äì10 cells per spot
üìä Integration: Compatible with Seurat, Scanpy, and Space Ranger pipelines.

Visium (10x Genomics)



Slide-seq

Slide-seq achieves higher spatial resolution using DNA-barcoded beads.

  • A surface is coated with thousands of 10 ¬µm beads, each with a unique positional barcode.
  • Tissue is transferred onto the bead array; mRNA diffuses and binds to nearby beads.
  • After sequencing, spatial positions are reconstructed using the known bead coordinates.
  • Enables near single-cell resolution across entire tissue sections.

What is the primary goal of the RNA-seq workflow?

  1. To physically sequence the entire length of the human genome.
  2. To quantify and compare transcript abundance across different samples.
  3. To align short reads to a Protein-Protein Interaction (PPI) network.
